Martin Luther King

The most visible spokesperson and leader in the civil rights movement from 1955.


Martin Luther King: time line

  • 1929 - Michael King is born in Atlanta. His father changes the boy’s name, as well as his own, to Martin Luther King several years later.
  • 1944 - King enrolls at Morehouse College after passing the entrance exam at age 15.
  • 1948 - King graduates from Morehouse College with bachelor’s degree in sociology.
  • 1955 - June 5: King earns doctorate from Boston University
    Dec. 5: King is named president of the Montgomery Improvement Association.
  • 1956 - King’s home is bombed while he is speaking at a meeting. His wife and daughter are unharmed.
  • 1960 - King moves from Montgomery to Atlanta to focus on the civil rights struggle.
  • 1962 - A member of the American Nazi Party hits King in the face twice at an SCLC conference in Birmingham.
  • 1964 - Time magazine names King “Man of the Year” for 1963.
    King wins Nobel Peace Prize.
  • 1967 - King unveils plans for a Poor People’s Campaign, a mass civil disobedience protest, for the spring in Washington. It was intended as an expansion of his civil rights activities into the area of economic rights
  • 1968 - King is shot and killed on the balcony of the Lorraine Motel.
